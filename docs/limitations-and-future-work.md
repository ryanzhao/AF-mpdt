# Limitations and Future Work

This document summarizes the current known limitations of the MPDT project and outlines potential directions for future improvements. The notes below are intended to help guide future design reviews, simulation updates, and experimental planning.

## Known Limitations

- The mechanical structure may still require further optimization to improve manufacturability, alignment, stiffness, thermal behavior, and ease of assembly.
- Thruster component material choices may need additional validation with materials that tolerate higher temperatures and provide stronger resistance to plasma erosion.
- The vacuum test environment, measurement accuracy, calibration process, and test repeatability still need improvement before results can be considered robust.
- The control panel and electrical connections need more complete safety documentation, including connection diagrams, operating procedures, interlocks, grounding, and emergency shutdown guidance.
- Simulation results may differ from real test results because of simplified assumptions, uncertain boundary conditions, material behavior, measurement error, and facility effects.

## Future Improvements

- Optimize the magnetic field layout to improve plasma acceleration, field uniformity, component packaging, and overall thruster performance.
- Improve the cathode and anode structure, including geometry, spacing, thermal management, erosion resistance, and serviceability.
- Improve the propellant feed system or gas-line interface to support safer setup, more stable flow control, clearer sealing requirements, and better repeatability.
- Add a more complete data acquisition system for voltage, current, pressure, temperature, flow rate, thrust, and timing data.
- Add a more detailed comparison between simulation and experiment, including test conditions, assumptions, uncertainty estimates, and observed differences.

## Safety Notes

- High current, high temperature components, vacuum systems, and plasma testing are all hazardous and can cause serious injury, equipment damage, fire, or other unsafe conditions.
- Do not operate hardware without appropriate supervision, safety reviews, personal protective equipment, insulation, grounding, emergency shutdown capability, and compliance with applicable laboratory procedures.
- The materials in this project are provided only for research and educational reference and are not a complete safety, manufacturing, or operating manual.
